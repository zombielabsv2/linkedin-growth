# Book Pipeline — Rahul Jindal

## Priority Order (by impact x differentiation)

### Book 1: "Organizational Metabolism"
**Subtitle:** How Enterprises Convert Intelligence Into Advantage
**Audience:** CXOs, transformation leaders, strategy consultants, AI leaders
**Differentiation:** Very High — genuinely original framework, no one else has it
**% Written:** ~40%
**Format:** Business book, 50K words. Start as LinkedIn series.

**Proposed Structure:**
1. The Metabolism Gap — why most AI transformations fail (74% stuck at pilots)
2. The Four Archetypes — Cosmetic → Assisted → Augmented → Adaptive
3. Workflow Debt — the hidden liability of accumulated process complexity
4. Verification Architecture — from checking every output to trusting systems
5. Layer Compression — why review layers persist and how to evolve them
6. Decision Traces — the new moat (state clock vs event clock)
7. The 7 Wonders of AI Value — beyond efficiency
8. The Metabolic Index — measuring readiness across 6 dimensions
9. The Abstraction Layer — why you don't need to chase every launch
10. Leadership Playbook — 5 commitments that drive metabolic rate
11. Case Studies — anonymized Google and enterprise examples
12. The Compounding Effect — why the gap is accelerating, not narrowing

**Seed posts:** Omnibus, Decision Traces, 7 Wonders, Five Force Multipliers, How to Land a Transformation, Gadfly/SVB, Don't Chase Every Launch, Rocks/Pebbles/Sand, Planning vs Battling

## Execution Strategy

**Phase 1 — LinkedIn Series (Now)**
Pick Book 1 or 2. Write 10-15 posts as a series. Each post = one chapter idea, grounded in a specific story, with a named framework. Test which resonate.

**Phase 2 — Newsletter/Long-form (Month 2-3)**
Convert best-performing posts into longer essays (Substack or LinkedIn articles). Build email list.

**Phase 3 — Manuscript (Month 4-8)**
Expand essays into chapters. Fill gaps (case studies, interviews, unwritten stories). First draft.

**Phase 4 — Publish (Month 9-12)**
Self-publish or pitch to publishers. Use LinkedIn audience as proof of demand.
