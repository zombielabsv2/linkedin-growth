## Post 1: Keeping the Gadfly Alive (SVB)

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_svb-siliconvalleybanked-activity-7040524302282096640-6TkE
**Date:** March 12, 2023
**Likes:** 167 | **Comments:** 18
**Hashtags:** #svb #SiliconValleyBanked

We all have come across the gadfly personalities. That person at our workplace who understands the workings of the system, has thought long and hard about what can go wrong, and who nobody likes - because they spoil the party and the mood!

They point to systemic deficiencies, they point to the unsustainable nature of decisions made, they speak of risks, they urge caution, they look unhappy and they are vilified!

In a pleasure seeking and delusional positivity seeking mob behaviour, the gadflies are sidelined, they get frustrated and they leave.

This often doesn't matter - the party becomes wilder - until it does matter. And when it does matter and when things go catastrophically wrong, just like the gadfly had pointed out - it is too late.

A 40+ year old institution can vanish in under 48 hours. The Silicon Valley Bank was without a Chief Risk Officer for many quarters. The business teams kept on piling risk. It didn't matter till the bonuses kept flowing and growing. And when it mattered, it was already too late.

It is said that, "good advice grates on the ears" - good advice given out of sincerity usually points to one's mistakes or weakness and therefore, is not easy to accept.

You can kill or avoid the gadfly in your team. You can get rid of that gadfly friend because they drain you.

Whose loss will it be?

Don't surround yourself with the monochrome palette of yes-men and yes-women.

Keep the gadfly alive. They might be the only ones who can save you!

## Post 3: Stakeholder Management

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_a-colleague-sought-my-inputs-on-stakeholder-activity-6912662767380426752-_wMT
**Date:** March 24, 2022
**Likes:** 243 | **Comments:** 22

A colleague sought my inputs on stakeholder management today, especially when playing an advisory & enablement role. Here's what I shared with them:

1. Train and develop yourself to start thinking from the perspective of the person you are stakeholder managing: 1) What is important to them 2) What level are they operating at - their vantage point 3) What energy & vocabulary do they use to describe organizational realities & opportunities

2. Most senior stakeholders constantly, but privately, worry about whether they are connected enough to the reality on the ground. In a trusted relationship, it may be helpful to share your perspective on the "Red", "Amber" and "Green" in their world - from your perspective (call it that)

3. Mask the complexity of your world - they don't have the cognitive space to deal with their complexity *and* your complexity. Be like google dot com - the UI has remained as a single textbox for 20+ years!

4. Don't share more than 2-3 things with an Exec stakeholder - they have way too many meetings in a week to process or register a longer list from you

5. Give them the confidence that you have things under control - reduce their list of things to worry about

6. Ask open ended questions when seeking their inputs. Closed ended questions will elicit no or not very helpful inputs and it would be a lost opportunity for you.

7. Seek feedback regularly and make it easy for them to give you the feedback

None of the above is easy. No one does a perfect job of all of the above, all the time. We are all learning!

## Post 4: How to Land a Transformation

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_leadership-transformation-activity-6832660186251964416-Qdfo
**Date:** August 15, 2021
**Likes:** 66 | **Comments:** 8
**Hashtags:** #leadership #transformation

How to land a transformation?

1. Start with a vision of what you want the transformation to be about
2. Gather data that will help you learn more about the context in which you will be operating
3. Work through quantitative and qualitative means to develop insights validating (or invalidating) your vision, and the need for the transformation
4. Build your own conviction first - it is going to be hard work! *You* need to be convinced!
5. Craft a pithy slogan about your vision
6. Repeat (your slogan), repeat, repeat, repeat, repeat, repeat, repeat; or else it will get lost in organizational chaos and not be available to those who need it to make decisions
7. Build specific interventions that all put together will be needed to accomplish the vision
8. Run tightly managed programs for each of the interventions
9. Celebrate early success to build and sustain momentum in the organization / market
10. Reflect on the learnings from the journey often and share with your team.

## Post 6: Expectation Mismatch to Exceptional Match

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_success-careers-activity-6981996941186412544-lEUl
**Date:** October 1, 2022
**Likes:** 114 | **Comments:** 11
**Hashtags:** #success #careers

Expectation mismatch to exceptional match

In raising our son, we realised that when our narrative changed from "Is our child fit for the environment at school?" to "Is the school environment fit for our child?" is when we truly understood what was needed for anyone to thrive.

Any environment with all its multisensory stimuli - the sights, the sounds, the odors, the tactile experiences, the edible and non edible textures & tastes - and the integration of it all - can be quite a lot. Support or judgement from peers can help them thrive or drown.

This is no different for a workplace - the hours, the emails, the meetings, the politics, the experiences - the strategy or lack of it - can be quite a lot. Support or judgement from peers can help us thrive or drown.

Some onboarding, some mentoring, some goal setting, some check-ins, some rewards & recognition and some developmental feedback can work well for most employees. The quality of these interventions, the attitude of the people providing these interventions and the general employee centricity can determine long term success for the employees and the company. The most important person who determines the long term success of the employee is their manager.

Poor, partial or pedantic quality of interventions will break the company and its culture. Such breakage may not be apparent in months or even quarters. It takes a while for the organizational cancer to rear its ugly head in the form of missed revenue goals, poor spend efficiency, weak customer satisfaction or languishing time to market.

The impact is not only the company's P&L and long term sustainability. Companies with poor or weak focus on employee development are hell for the careers of the employees themselves.

An environment that breeds anxiety is no good for you.

The next time you are looking for a role at a place: Don't only ask if I am fit for this place but also if this place is a fit for me.

Careers, happiness and a general sense of well being will truly thrive in a place that is fit for you.

An environment that breeds these will help you accelerate.

An expectation mismatch will cause burn out.
An exceptional match will make you shine brightly.

Shine, don't burn.

Go from anxious to accelerating.

## Post 8: Winter is the Time to Build

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_winter-is-the-time-to-build-no-company-and-activity-6971647726056873984-4z2B
**Date:** September 3, 2022
**Likes:** 160 | **Comments:** 8

Winter is the time to build. No company and no career can be unidirectionally 'up and to the right'. Company trajectories and career trajectories are sometimes discouragingly marred by painful periods of slump, and sometimes greatly supported by periods of good luck.

We tend to attribute the periods of good luck to our own hard work, and periods of slump as something imposed on us by something outside!

Agency is having a sense of control over life outcomes. Easy to say, difficult to practice. But, practice we must.

Periods of slump can feel like the label says. Slump.

A lot of folks, including me, have experienced periods of slump in our careers. And no one is immune to it.

My learning and, thus my, recommendation for dealing with a period of slump is to focus on building.

1. **Building faith:** We seek when we suffer. If suffering leads to seeking, then suffering isn't a bad thing. This isn't obvious and is very difficult to follow while the going is good. With Netflix, Amazon Prime, Tik Tok, YouTube Shorts and so on - who has the time to seek? Let this be that time.

2. **Building physical and mental health:** Burnout is real. Our body and mind needs time, space and effort to heal and recover. Food is medicine but we might have been eating just to get by. Sleep might have been sacrificed often so we can 'give our best'. There was never enough time to sleep 8 hours. Let this be that time.

3. **Building relationships:** We might have been accumulating thousands of LinkedIn contacts. Hundreds of folks from B School. Tens of friends from Engineering. Deepest BFF relationships from school. But, we never had the time to connect, reconnect and nourish those relationships. Let this be that time.

4. **Building skills:** When the career is going well in a stable company and a stable role, skill building takes a back seat. No function and no industry is staying static. We never have the time to fully catch up on the latest in our field of work or to contribute meaningfully and never have the time to really build skills that will help for the rest of our career. Let this be that time.

5. **Building a venture:** Irrespective of whether one was 'working 18 hours' (!) or not, there likely wasn't enough time, motivation or mental bandwidth to really think about building a side gig. Commercial or otherwise. Something that could become a real occupation down the line. We are not living in times when the start-to-finish of our careers will be one company or even one industry. Gig economy is here. Specialization is rewarded well. Never had the time so far to be a specialist outside the core field of work? Let this be that time.

Winter is coming. Or, Winter may be here already.

Faith, Health, Relationships, Skills or Venture. What will you build? Something else?

## Post 9: Coping and Thriving in a Suboptimal World

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_transformationishard-liveanotherday-activity-6965497542436339712-GTIi
**Date:** August 17, 2022
**Likes:** 73 | **Comments:** 7
**Hashtags:** #TransformationIsHard #LiveAnotherDay

Multiple recent conversations pointed in this direction. To a perceptive person, the world can come across as a (largely) suboptimal place. The more you perceive, the more that appears broken - the more that needs to be fixed. Attempts to try to fix it, and especially fix it all, and now, can feel daunting, de-energizing and discouraging. How is one to thrive in such a place?!

These three steps may help:
1. Answering this question: What in the "metrics that matter" is breaking because of what you have spotted? Is it *materially broken* when articulated in terms of metrics that matter?
2. Telling the story in terms of the key "metrics" that most people, and especially people with the power, will understand. Using ALL the forums available to you to tell the story. Trying several times, wisely.
3. Building an alliance of people who care about the same problem

This applies to sub-optimalities in the workplace, family, neighborhood, society or in the world at large.

Ultimately:
1. If nothing is *materially broken*, or
2. A convincing story cannot be told to mobilize the decision makers, or
3. No one else seems to care, then...

...accepting that the world is not a utopia may be the best answer for one's own mental health and longevity. You cannot fix all the problems. Surely, not today. Surely, not alone. You cannot stop perceiving but you can start accepting. Acceptance is also a solution.

## Post 11: Shadow of the Leader and Shade of the Team

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_shadow-of-the-leader-and-shade-of-the-team-activity-6930034033233903616-KJEy
**Date:** May 11, 2022
**Likes:** 35 | **Comments:** 3

Shadow of the Leader and Shade of the Team

It is well and often said that 'Leaders set the temperature' i.e. the mood, energy, approach, vocabulary, behaviours, priorities, decisions of a leader deeply influence their team - in ways big and small, in productive ways and counter-productive ways. This awareness is good in the hands of leaders trying to 'institutionalize' a change by changing how they approach a certain topic - in thoughts, words and actions. They change and slowly but surely the change percolates to their team members too.

Thermostat Leadership: Shadow of the Leader is real & good yet there's a need to be cautious!

I think it works in the other direction too - something I am calling the Shade of the Team. Leaders can constantly learn from their teams - professional and personal traits, ideas, knowledge, enthusiasm, innovation - and that is great.

For leaders leading large teams, or in general, with broader priorities beyond just the business of their team - the team's fears, anxieties, and agenda also significantly shape what their leaders *end up* believing to be the truth. The truth wrt the state of the business, role & performance of partners, opportunities and challenges, the mood, energy, and vocabulary. This awareness is good in the hands of leaders who might be (prematurely or belatedly) picking up on need for change in a partner org based on inputs from their team, (prematurely or belatedly) picking up on the need for change in strategy & resourcing and so on. As always, great to trust but verify!

Thermometer Leadership: Shade of the Team is comforting yet there's a need to be cautious!

Are you able to balance casting the Shadow and being in the Shade?

## Post 12: Rocks, Pebbles, Sand and Water

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_rocks-pebbles-sand-and-water-organizations-activity-6923467610894266369-K0iG
**Date:** April 23, 2022
**Likes:** 53 | **Comments:** 5

Rocks, Pebbles, Sand and Water

Organizations struggle with ever-expanding priority lists -- P0s through P4s -- while team attention spans shrink and talent grows fatigued. Identifying "rocks" (high-impact work) requires asking foundational questions:

1. Which key business metric does this address?
2. What's the magnitude of movement expected?
3. Is quantitative data supporting this (not just qualitative)?
4. Does it represent meaningful metric improvement?

A critical test: "Whose battle are we fighting?" If sponsorship lacks importance, it's a "pebble" instead.

Legitimate work connects to revenue growth, cost reduction, faster time-to-market, or stakeholder satisfaction. Everything else qualifies as "sand or water" -- organizational busywork deserving rejection.

"Busyness is not the same as business."

## Post 13: Doing a Global Role from India

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_doing-a-global-role-sitting-out-of-india-activity-6915957326784970752-irmM
**Date:** April 2, 2022
**Likes:** 362 | **Comments:** 17

Doing a global role sitting out of India can be quite challenging, but for those of us who are keen to be in India, here are some thoughts that might work:

1. **Lead with world class expertise:** Create and promote a culture that puts domain expertise and solution development & delivery expertise over everything else. Expertise can't be ignored for long.

2. **Execution excellence:** Obsess over your deliverables, major and minor deliverables - again, setting and upholding a world class standard is an unfailing recipe and consistency in execution excellence is bound to win support among the global stakeholders.

3. **Higher bar:** This is a fact. Hard as it may be. When workflows or responsibilities move from HQ or higher cost locations to outside HQ or lower cost locations, the expectation goes *up*. Hold yourself and others around you to a high bar, even higher than what it previously was. That is the only way to build a sustainable brand for the country.

4. **Communicate regularly, clearly and consistently:** "Distance breeds fear, anxiety and doubt." Communicate weekly, monthly and quarterly. Communicate tactical updates and your strategic vision. When a choice must be made between more and less - choose to over communicate.

5. **Visit your global stakeholders often:** Even more importantly, where possible, have them visit. The power of a week long, holistic immersive experience is immense and you have their attention the whole time as opposed to visiting them. Both are needed.

6. **Hire for the global corporation and hire well:** Never dilute, instead raise, the hiring standards. Never hire only for your role or only for your team. Hire for the global corporation and hire for the industry - attract great people to your industry and give them rich roles.

7. **Be an exporter of talent:** Every hire you make for the company (and even the industry) that goes and works in other parts of the organisation creates a brand for your team and location. A gift that keeps giving. Accept the need for an always on hiring pipeline but help the organisation and *also* the individuals by exporting top talent.

8. **Achieve an optimal global location mix:** Just because you are in a certain location certainly should not mean that everyone in your organization needs to be in the same location / time zone. Solve for business needs - hire globally, align to stakeholder locations, talent availability and hire a diverse team where every new individual enriches the team, enriches the org and enriches the culture. Great people are found everywhere!

What other strategies have worked for you? What must be avoided?

## Post 17: Return to Office / Future of Work

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rahuljindal_futureofwork-returntooffice-hybridwork-activity-6917372355455455232-R4cb
**Date:** April 6, 2022
**Likes:** 84 | **Comments:** 6
**Hashtags:** #futureofwork #returntooffice #hybridwork

The return of summer is the summer of return! That rhymes well in the Northern Hemisphere :) As Business takes on a pre-pandemic form and activity resumes to normal levels rapidly - as if we are picking up from where we left off in Feb 2020 - there are transitions to be made.

I have started going to the office and here are some realizations from the early days:

1. **Cognitive load:** Everyone's situation is different - for some of us, the cognitive load at work is much less and more predictable than at home! Non meetings work feels easier in the office - though, admittedly, it used to be the opposite pre-pandemic. Nothing's more fickle than the human mind!

2. **Need to learn how to work the water cooler again:** I mean it literally. Someone reconfigured the watercooler to now be operated using a foot pedal and I experienced a learning curve. This might also be true for the figurative water cooler :)

3. **Suspending judgement:** Everyone is dealing with something and most of the time we may not even know what it is. Much easier, and much more helpful to just suspend judgement on why someone's in the office vs remote, for how long, on which days and so on - honestly, none of our business.

4. **Making it work is everyone's responsibility:** Leaders have a role to play but it will certainly not work without *everyone* contributing routinely, and wholeheartedly, to making hybrid work, work.

5. **Return to office is not an on / off switch:** This is a significant change of established behaviours - both pre-pandemic and over the last two years. It will need time to make the transition well.

6. **Reconfigured responsibilities:** Most of us assumed (or were "voluntold") responsibilities at home over the last two years given our availability and these responsibilities will need arrangements to be made for.

7. **Future of work is here:** Let's all be the people participating in looking for the solution. It's a generational opportunity to shape and succeed in the Future of Work!

How are you settling in?
